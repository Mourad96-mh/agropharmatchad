not for where the planner happens to report volume.

## How to read the planner data

The export is **MAD (Morocco), France/Cameroun-weighted, with zero "tchad" terms** — because Chad's
search volume sits below Keyword Planner's reporting threshold, not because there's no market. So:

- **Do not chase the France/Cameroun terms** (`agriculture en france`, `technisem cameroun`, `les
graines de france`…). They carry volume but none of it is your customer.
- **Use the planner only as a vocabulary reference** — it tells us the exact words French-speaking
  growers type for seeds (`graines`, `semences potagères`, `graine de tomate`, `plants F1`,
  `germination`, `culture de tomate`). We reuse that vocabulary, attached to **Chad geo-modifiers**.

## The three real levers for a Chad local business (in priority order)

1. **Google Business Profile + NAP consistency.** This is the single biggest lever — far more than
   any keyword. The GBP exists (`/g/11k9fn3yvd`); make its name/address/phone/hours **identical**
   to the site, and it currently shows a `fr-MA` (Morocco) locale that should read Chad.
2. **Local geo signals on-page.** Every page already carries "au Tchad"; the LocalBusiness schema
   now lists `areaServed` = Tchad + N'Djamena, Moundou, Sarh, Abéché, Doba, Bongor, with geo coords.
3. **Branded discovery.** Real phone/WhatsApp in `site.js` so "agropharma tchad" searches convert.

## Catalogue / content decisions (Chad context)

- **Maïs** has volume in the export but it's France/Cameroun, and it's a **field crop** outside our
  maraîcher catalogue. In Chad maize _is_ grown — so this is a real **catalogue question**: do you
  sell maize seed? If yes, it's worth a page; if no, ignore the planner volume.
- **Graines de fleurs** — France-driven, not a Chad priority. Skip.
- The `/guides/` blog stays the main organic lever, but keep every guide **anchored to Chad**
  (local seasons, local pests, N'Djamena market) so it earns the few local + Francophone-Sahel reads.
